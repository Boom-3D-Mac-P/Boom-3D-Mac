## 📄 Overview

<a href="#boom3D">Boom 3D</a> serves Mac users who are dissatisfied with the audio quality from Mac's built-in speakers, who want better headphone listening without purchasing a hardware equalizer, and who want the spatial audio experience on stereo content without headphones that support Apple's spatial audio natively.

The <a href="#boom3D">MacBook built-in speaker use case</a> applies EQ and enhancement to compensate for the physical limitations of MacBook's small built-in speakers — boosting frequency ranges that the small speakers reproduce poorly and applying the 3D widening effect to create a more expansive audio presentation from hardware constrained by chassis size. The <a href="#boom3D">headphone listening optimization use case</a> applies equalization tuned for specific headphone models, or general headphone profiles that improve the frequency response characteristics of typical headphones — addressing the bass-heavy or treble-harsh response that many headphones produce without correction.

The <a href="#boom3D">home theater Mac use case</a> connects a Mac to an external speaker system and uses Boom 3D's equalization and spatial processing to optimize the audio for the room and speaker placement. <a href="#boom3D">Volume inadequacy use case</a> addresses the specific frustration of Mac audio being insufficient in louder environments — the volume booster provides additional amplification beyond what the system slider alone achieves.

<a href="#boom3D">Genre-specific EQ presets</a> provide named equalizer configurations optimized for different music styles — classical, jazz, hip-hop, electronic, rock, and other genres each with EQ curves reflecting the frequency priorities of that style. <a href="#boom3D">Spatial audio fidelity settings</a> adjust the intensity of the 3D processing — subtle widening for natural-sounding stereo enhancement through to dramatic surround expansion for immersive listening.

<a href="#boom3D">Global 3D effect toggle</a> enables and disables all Boom 3D processing with a single click — comparing processed and unprocessed audio directly. <a href="#boom3D">Global Deejay Inc. development</a> maintains Boom 3D with updates for macOS compatibility and audio driver maintenance as macOS audio architecture evolves.
